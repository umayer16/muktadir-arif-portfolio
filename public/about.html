About Me

From problems to Intelligent and Systematic solutions

My journey into tech began with a simple frustration. When I moved from my hometown to Dhaka for my higher studies, I moved into a hostel with 300+ students and immediately saw the chaos of managing finances in such a crowded environment.

Instead of just complaining, I built the Hostel Expense Tracker — a fully functional web app now used by 70+ students daily to manage their budgets. That project taught me that the best software solves real problems for real people.

Since then, I've expanded far beyond web apps. I competed at the Gema International Robotics Olympiad 2026, earning the Orwell Award for the Circuit Crusaders project — an autonomous firefighting robot with multi-directional flame sensors and hands-free fire suppression. I've also co-authored VibeBench, an open-source benchmarking framework for holistic LLM evaluation.

I've rigorously upskilled through Harvard's CS50x and CS50 Python, and I'm an active Executive Committee Member at both Scintilla Science Club and the Josephite IT Club.

My Goal: To study Computer Science at a top global university and contribute to ethical, accessible AI research — building systems that matter from the ground up.
