Building from
First Principles.

My entry into computing wasn't theoretical — it was a hostel with 300+ students and a broken system for tracking shared expenses. I built the fix. That product is now used by 70+ students every day, and it taught me the most important lesson early: the best software solves real problems for real people.

That conviction has shaped everything since. I don't build demos. I build systems.

Research Focus

My current primary research is a vulnerability triage framework submitted to IEEE TDSC (Impact Factor ~7.3). The paper — "Beyond Severity Scores: Authentication-Aware Vulnerability Triage Using Contextual Risk Adjustment" — proposes an Adjusted Triage Score that moves beyond raw CVSS values to integrate real-world exploitability signals from CISA KEV and EPSS. I also co-authored VibeBench, an open-source LLM benchmarking framework submitted to JOSS.
